§ 01

Approach & Philosophy

Most people treat AI like a hammer looking for nails. I treat it like a consultant's toolkit — something you pick up only after you've understood the problem deeply.

At Actin Technologies, I joined as an intern and became their first AI team member within weeks — not because I knew every framework, but because I could sit across from a client, map their operational pain, and translate it into something buildable. That combination of structured thinking and technical depth is what I bring to every engagement.

I built Brainwave — a multi-agent enterprise intelligence platform integrating SAP, Zoho, and Salesforce — from whiteboard to production. I built a GraphRAG maintenance assistant that handled relationship-dense industrial data that naive vector search couldn't touch. Each project started not with a model choice, but with a business question.

"The best AI solutions come from people who understand both the boardroom conversation and the database schema."

Now at UMD Smith, I'm formalizing that instinct — studying systems analysis, data strategy, and the organizational dynamics that determine whether an AI initiative succeeds or quietly dies in a sandbox.
